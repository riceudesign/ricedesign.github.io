USING PHOTOSHOP MASKS: A WORKSHOP

Have you ever been halfway through editing a masterpiece before realizing you made a mistake somewhere? Yet, after repeatedly hitting the "undo" button, you realize Photoshop can only remember so much and
you are now stuck trying to finagle your way out of a mistake? There's a better way!
Non-Destructive Editing is a work methodology that focuses on making changes without overwriting the original data of an image.
In this workshop, Christina Zhou will go over the basics of Non-Destructive Editing,
focusing on how to use the two most popular methods of masking within Photoshop: Layer masks and Clipping masks.

HOW TO GET INTO DESIGN W/ TINA LIU

Tina is a senior from Sid studying Sociology. From branding to visual journalism to product design, Tina's been designing since she was a sophomore in high school. After switching her major 3 times, as a sophomore,
she decided to finally make the jump from being a pre-med to a product designer.
She's worked as an Art Director of the Rice Thresher, a Rice HCI research assistant, a UX design intern
